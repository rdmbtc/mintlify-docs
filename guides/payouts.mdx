When a market closes and a resolution is declared, Puls automatically calculates and distributes payouts to every trader who held a winning position. The process is fully automated — you don't need to submit a claim or take any action. Here's how it works, when to expect your funds, and how to move your winnings to your bank account.

## How Payouts Work

Puls uses a simple, fixed payout model: every winning share pays out exactly **$1.00** at resolution.

Your **gross payout** is calculated as:

```
Payout = Shares held × $1.00
```

Your **net profit** is the payout minus what you originally paid for those shares:

```
Net profit = Payout − Original cost
```

For example, if you bought 200 YES shares at $0.55 each (total cost: $110.00) and the market resolves YES, you receive $200.00 — a net profit of $90.00.

A small platform fee is applied to winning payouts. The fee percentage is shown in your trade summary before you confirm any trade, so you always know your exact net return in advance. Losing positions — shares on the side that did not win — expire worthless. No portion of a losing position is returned.

## Payout Timeline

Once a market is officially resolved, Puls processes payouts automatically. Funds are credited to your **Puls balance** within **24 hours** of the resolution being declared.

In most cases, payouts are processed within a few hours. You'll receive a notification when your balance has been updated. You can also check the **Resolved Positions** tab in your portfolio to see the payout amount for any specific trade.

If more than 24 hours have passed since a market resolved and you haven't received your payout, contact Puls Support from **Account > Help**.

## Withdrawing Funds

Once your winnings are credited to your Puls balance, you can withdraw them to your linked bank account or payment method at any time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Withdrawal Page">
    Go to **Account > Funding > Withdraw**. Your current available balance is displayed at the top of the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter an Amount">
    Type the amount you want to withdraw. You can withdraw any amount up to your full available balance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a Withdrawal Method">
    Choose your preferred method from the options linked to your account — bank transfer (ACH) or debit card. If you haven't linked a payment method yet, follow the on-screen prompt to add one before proceeding.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and Confirm">
    Check the withdrawal summary, then click **Confirm Withdrawal**. You'll receive a confirmation notification once the request is submitted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Processing times** vary by method:

- **Debit card** — Typically 1–3 business days
- **Bank transfer (ACH)** — Typically 3–5 business days

Withdrawals are subject to identity verification requirements. If your account hasn't been fully verified, you may be prompted to complete verification before your first withdrawal is processed.

<Note>
  Your current available balance — including any recent payout credits — is always visible at **Account > Funding**. This is the amount you can use for new trades or withdraw to your bank.
</Note>

## Cancelled Markets (N/A Resolution)

Occasionally, a market cannot be resolved as YES or NO — for example, if the underlying event is postponed indefinitely, data becomes unavailable, or the question is rendered unanswerable. In these cases, the market resolves as **N/A**.

When a market resolves N/A, every trader receives a **full refund** of their original investment, regardless of which side they held. No fees are charged on N/A resolutions. Refunds are credited to your Puls balance within 24 hours, following the same timeline as standard payouts.

You can see the resolution status and your refund amount in the **Resolved Positions** tab of your portfolio.

<Tip>
  Once your payout lands in your balance, it's immediately available to trade. Reinvesting your winnings into new markets is a fast way to keep your capital working — head to the [Markets Feed](/guides/browsing-markets) to find your next opportunity.
</Tip>
