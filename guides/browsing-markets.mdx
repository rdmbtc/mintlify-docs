Puls gives you access to hundreds of live prediction markets across politics, sports, finance, and more. Whether you know exactly what you're looking for or want to explore what's trending, the markets feed makes it easy to find opportunities that match your interests and trading style.

## The Markets Feed

The Markets Feed is your home base on Puls — the main view you land on after logging in. It displays all currently active markets in a scrollable list, updated in real time as probabilities shift and new markets open.

From the feed, you can browse every open market at a glance. Markets near their closing date are flagged so you can act before trading ends. As prices move throughout the day, the feed reflects the latest crowd-implied probabilities, giving you a live pulse on what the market thinks will happen next.

Use the top navigation to switch between featured markets handpicked by the Puls team and the full all-markets view.

## Filtering and Searching

Narrow the feed down to exactly what you want using Puls's filtering and search tools.

**Filter by category** — Select one or more categories from the filter bar at the top of the feed to show only markets in that space:

- **Politics** — Elections, legislation, approval ratings, and geopolitical events
- **Sports** — Game outcomes, season results, awards, and player milestones
- **Finance** — Stock movements, economic indicators, and company events
- **Science & Tech** — Product launches, research breakthroughs, and tech milestones
- **Entertainment** — Awards shows, box office results, and pop culture moments
- **World Events** — Breaking news and global affairs

**Search by keyword** — Use the search bar to find markets by any word or phrase in the market question. Results update as you type, so you can quickly zero in on a topic like "Fed rate" or "World Cup."

**Sort the feed** — Use the sort dropdown to reorder markets by:

- **Volume** — Highest-traded markets first, useful for finding the most active opportunities
- **Recency** — Newest markets at the top, great for spotting fresh questions
- **Probability** — Sort by YES probability to find markets near 50/50 or heavily skewed outcomes
- **Closing Soon** — Markets expiring soonest at the top

<Tip>
  Sort by **Closing Soon** to surface markets that are about to resolve. These can offer urgent opportunities if you have a strong view before the window closes.
</Tip>

## Market Cards

Each market in the feed is displayed as a card showing the most important information at a glance. Here's what you'll see on every card:

- **Question** — The plain-language question the market resolves on, e.g., *"Will the Fed raise rates in September?"*
- **Current probability** — The market-implied YES probability, expressed as a percentage. This reflects what traders collectively believe is the likelihood of a YES outcome.
- **Volume** — The total amount traded in this market so far. Higher volume generally means tighter spreads and more price discovery.
- **Closing date** — The date and time when trading ends. After this point, no new positions can be opened.

Click any card to open the full market detail page, where you'll find the resolution criteria, full price history, order book, and the option to place a trade.

## Following Markets

If you find a market you want to keep an eye on but aren't ready to trade yet, you can follow it to get updates.

To follow a market, open the market detail page and click the **Follow** button (the bookmark icon near the market title). Followed markets are saved to your **Watchlist**, accessible from your account menu.

When you follow a market, you'll receive notifications when:

- The probability moves significantly
- Trading is closing soon
- The market resolves

To manage your notification preferences or remove markets from your watchlist, go to **Account > Notifications**.
