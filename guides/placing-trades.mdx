Trading on Puls means buying shares in the outcome you believe will happen. Every market has two sides — YES and NO — and the price of each share reflects the crowd's current estimate of that outcome's probability. When the market resolves, winning shares pay out $1.00 each. Here's how to go from finding a market to executing your first trade.

<Warning>
  Always read the **resolution criteria** before placing a trade. Markets resolve strictly according to their stated criteria, which may differ from how the broader news covers the event. Misunderstanding the criteria is one of the most common reasons traders are surprised by an outcome.
</Warning>

## Placing Your Trade

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a Market">
    Start by finding a market you want to trade. Browse the Markets Feed, use the search bar, or filter by category to find a question you have a strong view on. Click the market card to open the full market detail page.

    If you need help navigating the feed, see the [Browsing and Discovering Markets](/guides/browsing-markets) guide.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review the Market">
    Before committing any funds, take a moment to review the market details:

    - **Question** — Confirm you understand exactly what is being predicted.
    - **Resolution criteria** — Read how and when the market will be resolved. This defines what counts as YES or NO.
    - **Closing date** — Note when trading ends. You must place your trade before this deadline.
    - **Current probability** — The live YES probability tells you how the market is currently priced. A 70% YES means each YES share costs approximately $0.70.

    Use the price history chart to see how the probability has moved over time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Your Position">
    Once you're ready to trade, click either the **YES** or **NO** button in the trade panel on the right side of the market page.

    - Choose **YES** if you believe the event will happen.
    - Choose **NO** if you believe it will not happen.

    The trade panel will update to show the current price per share for your chosen side.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Your Amount">
    Type the dollar amount you want to invest in the amount field. As you type, the trade panel updates in real time to show:

    - **Number of shares** you'll receive at the current price
    - **Price per share** at the time of your order
    - **Potential payout** if your position wins at resolution
    - **Potential return** expressed as a percentage gain on your investment

    You can also enter a specific number of shares and let the system calculate the cost. Use the toggle above the input field to switch between dollar amount and share quantity.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review and Confirm">
    Before submitting, review the trade summary displayed in the panel:

    - **Shares** — The number of shares you are buying
    - **Price per share** — The rate you're paying, locked in at the moment you confirm
    - **Total cost** — The amount that will be deducted from your Puls balance
    - **Potential payout** — What you receive if you win (shares × $1.00)
    - **Fees** — Any applicable platform fees are shown here before you commit

    When everything looks correct, click **Confirm Trade**. Your balance will be debited immediately and your position will appear in your portfolio.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding Your Trade Summary

After confirming, Puls generates a trade summary. Here's what each line means:

- **Shares purchased** — The number of outcome shares you now hold. Each share is worth $1.00 if your side wins at resolution.
- **Price per share** — The probability-based price you paid, between $0.01 and $0.99. A YES share priced at $0.65 implies a 65% market probability of YES.
- **Total cost** — The total amount debited from your balance (shares × price per share, plus any fees).
- **Potential payout** — Your maximum return if the market resolves in your favor. This equals the number of shares you hold, since each winning share pays $1.00.
- **Potential return** — Your net profit if you win: potential payout minus total cost.
- **Fees** — Puls charges a small percentage fee on winning payouts. The fee is displayed before you confirm so there are no surprises.

## After You Trade

Once your trade is confirmed, your new position immediately appears in your portfolio under **Open Positions**. A few things to keep in mind:

- **Prices move** — The probability and share price will continue to fluctuate as other traders buy and sell. Your original cost is locked in, but the current market value of your position may rise or fall before resolution.
- **No action required to win** — If the market resolves in your favor, your payout is credited to your Puls balance automatically. You don't need to do anything.
- **Early exit** — Depending on the market, you may be able to sell your shares before resolution. See the portfolio guide for details.

<Note>
  Your open positions, trade history, and current portfolio value are all visible in the **Portfolio** section of your account. Visit the [Portfolio guide](/guides/portfolio) to learn how to track and manage your trades.
</Note>
