Understanding how and when you get paid is just as important as knowing how to trade. This page covers everything from the moment a market resolves to the point your funds land in your bank account — including what happens in edge cases like cancelled markets and delayed payouts.

<Accordion title="When do I receive my payout after a market resolves?">
  Payouts are credited to your Puls account balance automatically, usually within a few minutes of a market being officially resolved. You'll receive an in-app notification and an email confirmation when your winnings have been applied. From there, your balance is immediately available to reinvest in other markets or withdraw to your bank account.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How much is each winning share worth?">
  Every winning share on Puls pays out exactly $1.00 at resolution. If you bought YES shares at $0.65 each and the market resolves YES, you receive $1.00 per share — a profit of $0.35 per share before any applicable fees. Losing shares expire worthless at $0.00.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How do I withdraw my funds?">
  Go to **Account → Wallet → Withdraw** and select your preferred withdrawal method — bank transfer, debit card, or another supported option in your region. Enter the amount you'd like to withdraw, confirm the details, and submit. You may be asked to complete an additional identity verification step if you haven't done so already.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="How long does a withdrawal take?">
  Withdrawal processing times vary by method. Debit card withdrawals typically arrive within 1–3 business days, while bank transfers can take 3–5 business days depending on your financial institution. You'll receive a confirmation email when your withdrawal has been processed on our end, and you can track its status in the **Wallet** section of your account.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What happens if a market is cancelled (N/A resolution)?">
  Occasionally a market cannot be resolved — for example, if the underlying event is postponed indefinitely or reliable source data is unavailable. In these cases, the market is resolved as N/A and all participants are refunded the full amount they paid for their shares. Refunds are credited to your Puls account balance and are usually processed within 24 hours of the cancellation.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are there withdrawal fees?">
  Puls may charge a fee for certain withdrawal methods — check our Fees page for the current schedule before initiating a transfer. In addition, your bank or payment provider may apply their own processing fees, which are outside our control. We recommend reviewing both sources so you have a clear picture of any costs before you withdraw.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is the minimum withdrawal amount?">
  The minimum withdrawal amount is $10.00. This threshold helps keep processing costs manageable and ensures that transfers are worthwhile for you after any third-party fees. If your balance is below $10.00, you can continue trading to build it up before initiating a withdrawal.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Do I need to report my winnings for tax purposes?">
  Tax obligations on trading profits vary significantly by jurisdiction and personal circumstances. In many countries, winnings from prediction markets may be considered taxable income or capital gains, but the rules differ widely depending on where you live. We strongly recommend consulting a qualified tax professional in your region to understand your specific obligations and ensure you remain compliant with local tax law.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Why hasn't my payout arrived yet?">
  If your payout is taking longer than expected, first check the **Wallet** section of your account to confirm the withdrawal status. Delays can occasionally occur due to bank processing times, additional identity verification requirements, or high withdrawal volumes on our platform. If your payout is more than 5 business days overdue, please contact our support team at support@pulsmarket.tech and we'll investigate promptly.
</Accordion>
