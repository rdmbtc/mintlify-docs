Prediction markets on Puls are structured around a simple question: will this event happen? Every market has two possible outcomes — YES or NO — and the price of each outcome at any given moment reflects the crowd's collective estimate of the probability. Understanding how markets are created, how prices move, and how payouts are distributed will help you trade with confidence.

## The Basics

Each market on Puls revolves around a binary outcome. When you trade, you're buying shares in one of two positions:

- **YES shares** — you believe the event will happen.
- **NO shares** — you believe the event will not happen.

Prices are expressed on a scale from **0 to 100**, representing a percentage probability. A YES price of 65 means the market collectively estimates a 65% chance the event occurs. If you buy YES at 65 and the event happens, your shares resolve at 100 and you profit. If the event doesn't happen, your shares resolve at 0 and you lose your stake. NO shares work the same way in reverse — a NO price is always 100 minus the current YES price.

## Market Lifecycle

Every market on Puls moves through a defined set of stages from creation to final settlement.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Market Opens">
    A new market is created with a clear question, resolution criteria, and a closing date. The initial price is set based on the starting probability estimate, and trading opens to all users.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Traders Buy Shares">
    Users buy YES or NO shares based on their predictions. As more traders buy into a position, the price of that position rises, pushing the opposing position's price down. Prices continuously reflect the shifting consensus of all active traders.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Market Closes">
    Trading closes at the pre-defined cutoff — typically when the real-world event is about to occur or has occurred. After the market closes, no new positions can be opened or modified, but existing positions are held until resolution.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Event Resolves">
    Once the real-world outcome is confirmed, Puls resolves the market to YES or NO based on official sources and the pre-defined resolution criteria. The resolution is applied to all open positions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Payouts Distributed">
    Winning shares are settled at a value of 100 and the proceeds are credited to your Puls balance instantly. Losing shares are settled at 0. Your full trading history and P&L are updated in your portfolio.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## How Prices Are Set

Puls uses an **automated market maker (AMM)** to ensure that there is always liquidity in every market. Unlike a traditional exchange where your trade only goes through when someone else takes the other side, the AMM means you can always buy or sell shares at the current market price — no waiting for a counterparty.

As traders buy YES shares, the YES price rises and the NO price falls. As traders buy NO shares, the opposite happens. Over time, prices converge toward the true probability of the outcome — a phenomenon known as the *wisdom of the crowd*. Markets with more active traders tend to produce more accurate prices because they aggregate information from a larger pool of informed participants.

## Resolution

Every market on Puls is resolved based on clear, objective criteria that are defined before the market opens. Resolution sources typically include:

- **Official government or institutional announcements** (for political or economic markets)
- **Official league or sports body results** (for sports markets)
- **Verified news sources** (for breaking-news or current-events markets)

If an event's outcome is ambiguous or the resolution criteria cannot be met, Puls may extend the resolution window or, in rare cases, void the market and return funds to traders. You can always review the resolution criteria for any market before you trade.

<Tip>
  If you're new to prediction markets, start with small trade sizes while you get familiar with how prices move and how markets resolve. Building intuition with low-stakes trades is the fastest way to become a confident trader.
</Tip>
