Resolution is the process by which Puls determines the final outcome of a market and distributes payouts to shareholders. It's the moment the question gets answered. Every market on Puls is designed with resolution in mind from the outset — clear criteria, named sources, and a defined timeline ensure that the process is transparent, objective, and consistent for everyone involved.

## Resolution Criteria

Before a market opens for trading, Puls defines precise, objective criteria that specify exactly what must happen for the market to resolve YES. These criteria are written to be unambiguous, so there's no room for interpretation after the fact.

Good resolution criteria share a few characteristics:

- **Specificity** — They name an exact threshold, event, or declaration (e.g. "The Federal Reserve announces a rate cut of at least 25 basis points at its September meeting").
- **Verifiability** — The outcome can be confirmed using publicly available, authoritative information.
- **Time-boundedness** — They specify the period within which the event must occur to count as YES.

You can always read the full resolution criteria for any market by opening the market detail page before you trade.

## Resolution Sources

Puls resolves markets using authoritative, publicly accessible sources appropriate to the subject matter. The resolution source is declared on every market page so you know in advance where the final call will come from.

Common resolution sources include:

- **Official government and regulatory publications** for political and financial markets.
- **Major newswire services** (such as Reuters or AP) for breaking news events.
- **Official league or governing body records** for sports outcomes.
- **Verified company filings and press releases** for corporate and technology markets.
- **Peer-reviewed publications or official agency announcements** for science and health markets.

Puls does not use a single source for every market — the source is always matched to the nature of the event being tracked.

## Resolution Timeline

Once a market closes, the resolution process follows a structured sequence.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Market Closes">
    Trading halts at the scheduled closing date and time. No new positions can be opened or closed once this point is reached.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolution Team Reviews">
    The Puls resolution team checks the declared resolution source to determine the outcome. They verify that the criteria have been met (or not met) in a clear and objective manner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Outcome Confirmed">
    The outcome — YES, NO, or N/A — is confirmed and recorded. The market status updates to **Resolved** on the platform.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Shares Settled">
    Winning shares are marked for payout at $1.00 per share. Losing shares are settled at $0.00.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Payouts Distributed">
    Funds are credited to the accounts of all winning shareholders. You'll see the payout reflected in your balance immediately after settlement completes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Resolution typically completes within 24–48 hours after the underlying event occurs, provided the outcome is clearly determinable from the declared source. Complex or contested events may take longer.
</Note>

## Resolution Outcomes

Every market resolves to one of three outcomes:

- **YES** — The event described in the market question occurred and met the stated criteria. YES shareholders receive $1.00 per share; NO shareholders receive $0.00.
- **NO** — The event did not occur, or did not meet the stated criteria within the defined timeframe. NO shareholders receive $1.00 per share; YES shareholders receive $0.00.
- **N/A (Cancelled)** — The market is declared invalid, typically because the event became impossible to resolve fairly (for example, the event was cancelled, the resolution source ceased to exist, or the criteria contained an error). When a market resolves N/A, **all traders receive a full refund** of their original investment, regardless of position.

## Disputes

If you believe a market has been resolved incorrectly — for example, because the outcome does not match what the resolution source actually states — you can submit a dispute through the platform. Disputes are reviewed by a separate escalation team that re-examines the source material and the stated criteria independently of the original resolution decision.

To be considered, a dispute must:

- Reference the specific resolution criteria as written on the market page.
- Cite the relevant section of the declared resolution source.
- Be submitted within the dispute window shown on the resolved market page.

Puls is committed to accurate resolution and will correct confirmed errors, including reversing payouts and re-settling shares where necessary.
