A market on Puls is a question about a real-world event with a definite, verifiable outcome. You trade on what you believe will happen — and the collective activity of all traders produces a live probability that reflects the crowd's best guess at any given moment. Every market has a closing date, a resolution source, and a clear yes-or-no answer waiting to be revealed.

## Market Structure

Each market on Puls is built around five core elements:

- **Title** — A concise, unambiguous question such as "Will the Federal Reserve cut rates before July?"
- **Question / Event** — The underlying real-world event the market tracks, with precise criteria defining what counts as a YES outcome.
- **YES / NO Shares** — The two tradeable positions. Buying YES means you believe the event will happen; buying NO means you believe it won't.
- **Closing Date** — The date and time after which no new trades can be placed. This is usually set just before the event is expected to occur.
- **Category** — A topic label that helps you browse and filter markets across the platform.

## Reading a Market

When you open a market page, you'll see three key pieces of information at a glance:

- **Probability** — Displayed as a percentage such as "72% YES". This is the current implied probability derived from the live share price. A price of 72 cents per YES share means the market collectively estimates a 72% chance the event resolves YES.
- **Volume** — The total value of all trades placed in the market so far. Higher volume generally signals stronger consensus and more reliable pricing.
- **Traders** — The number of unique participants who have taken a position. More traders typically means the probability reflects a wider range of informed opinions.

As new information emerges and traders update their views, the probability shifts in real time — making the market page a living forecast you can watch and act on.

## Market Status

A market moves through a defined lifecycle from creation to final payout.

| Status | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Open** | Trading is active. You can buy or sell YES and NO shares. |
| **Closed** | The closing date has passed. No new trades can be placed; existing positions are locked. |
| **Resolving** | The resolution team is reviewing the outcome against the stated criteria. |
| **Resolved** | The outcome has been confirmed and share payouts have been distributed. |
| **Cancelled (N/A)** | The market was declared invalid — for example, because the underlying event was cancelled or could not be fairly determined. All traders receive a full refund of their original investment. |
